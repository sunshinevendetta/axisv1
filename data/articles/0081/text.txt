Vaportrap is a subgenre of electronic music that emerged in the early to mid-2010s, blending elements of vaporwave and trap music. It combines the nostalgic, lo-fi aesthetics of vaporwave with the hard-hitting beats and rhythmic patterns of trap, creating a sound that is both dreamy and grounded in contemporary hip-hop culture.

Vaportrap typically features slowed-down samples, reverb-heavy synths, and ethereal melodies that evoke a sense of nostalgia, similar to vaporwave. However, these elements are paired with the sharp, crisp drums, booming 808 bass, and hi-hat rolls that are characteristic of trap music. The result is a genre that feels both futuristic and retro, with a strong emphasis on atmosphere and mood.

Artists like Blank Banshee and Yung Lean are often associated with the vaportrap sound, although they each bring their unique influences to the genre. Blank Banshee’s albums, particularly *Blank Banshee 0* and *Blank Banshee 1*, are seminal works in vaportrap, blending glitchy, pixelated aesthetics with trap beats. Yung Lean, a Swedish rapper and member of the Sad Boys collective, also incorporated vaportrap elements into his music, contributing to the genre’s global reach.

Vaportrap is closely tied to internet culture, with much of its music and associated visuals circulating through online platforms like Bandcamp, SoundCloud, and Tumblr. The genre’s visual style often includes glitch art, retro computer graphics, and a blend of surreal and nostalgic imagery that complements its sonic characteristics.

Essential tracks:

- "Fiji Water" by Soudiere: A track that embodies the vaportrap style, combining ambient, dreamlike synths with punchy trap rhythms.
- "Ginseng Strip 2002" by Yung Lean: A track that fuses melancholic melodies with trap beats, showcasing the emotional depth that vaportrap can achieve.
- "Ammonia Clouds" by Blank Banshee: Another key track that highlights the genre’s atmospheric production and innovative blend of styles.
