Beach House is an electronic music subgenre that emerged in the late 1990s and early 2000s, primarily influenced by the sounds of house music, Balearic beat, and chill-out music. It is characterized by its relaxed, laid-back vibe, often incorporating elements of deep house, jazz, soul, and ambient music. Beach House music is designed to evoke the feeling of being at a beach or coastal setting, making it popular in seaside clubs, lounges, and sunset parties.

The genre gained significant popularity through compilations released by labels like Hed Kandi, which played a pivotal role in defining the Beach House sound. These compilations typically feature smooth, melodic tracks with lush instrumentation, including soft percussion, gentle basslines, and breezy synths. The music often blends organic sounds, such as acoustic guitars or saxophones, with electronic beats, creating a warm and inviting atmosphere.

Beach House music is also closely associated with the Balearic music scene in Ibiza, where it became a staple of the island's sunset bars and beach clubs. The genre's ability to create a sense of relaxation and escape has made it a favorite for both casual listening and more upscale, sophisticated environments.

Key tracks and compilations from Hed Kandi and other similar labels have cemented Beach House's reputation as the go-to soundtrack for coastal and summer settings, offering a musical experience that is both soothing and invigorating
