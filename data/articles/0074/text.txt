Deep house is a subgenre of house music that emerged in the 1980s, combining elements of Chicago house, jazz, and soul. Known for its slower tempo, deep basslines, and emphasis on mood and atmosphere, deep house music distinguishes itself from other house music styles with its more emotive and melodic structure. Tracks typically feature soft, rolling beats around 120-125 BPM, with a focus on layers of sound that create a soothing yet engaging listening experience.

The origins of deep house can be traced back to Chicago and Detroit, where pioneering producers such as Larry Heard (Mr. Fingers) and Marshall Jefferson began experimenting with deeper, more complex sounds in their house music productions. Larry Heard's track "Can You Feel It" (1986) is often cited as one of the defining tracks that helped shape the deep house sound, blending soulful melodies with subtle, rhythmic beats.

By the early 1990s, deep house had begun to spread to the UK and Europe, where it became a key influence in the development of electronic dance music. The genre retained a soulful feel, often incorporating jazzy chords, atmospheric pads, and smooth vocals. Producers such as Kerri Chandler, Masters At Work (Louie Vega and Kenny Dope), and Ron Trent further cemented the genre's sound, infusing house music with elements of soul, funk, and jazz.

Deep house enjoyed mainstream success in the early 2010s, with a new generation of producers bringing fresh sounds to the genre. Artists like Disclosure, Maya Jane Coles, and Jamie Jones helped to popularize deep house among a younger audience, fusing it with modern production techniques while still retaining the core elements of the genre.

Today, deep house remains a staple in clubs and festivals worldwide, appreciated for its smooth, hypnotic beats and emotional depth. It continues to evolve, absorbing influences from techno, ambient, and other electronic music styles.

Essential tracks:

- "Can You Feel It" by Mr. Fingers: One of the most influential tracks in the genre, defining the early sound of deep house with its soulful, atmospheric feel.
- "To Be In Love " by Masters At Work: A classic that bridges the gap between deep house and the mainstream, with its catchy hooks and uplifting melodies.
- "The Sun Can't Compare" by Larry Heard presents Mr. White: A track that captures the emotive essence of deep house with its deep grooves and ethereal vocals.
