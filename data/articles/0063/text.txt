Thunderdome is a legendary Dutch music festival and brand dedicated to hardcore and gabber music, and it has been an integral part of the electronic dance music scene since its inception in 1992. Created by ID\&T, the same organization behind events like Mysteryland and Sensation, Thunderdome emerged as a cultural movement that defined the harder styles of dance music.

The very first Thunderdome event was held on October 3, 1992, at the Thialf ice rink in Heerenveen, the Netherlands. It featured a massive octopus fairground ride, and the event's underground success set the stage for what would become one of the most iconic hardcore music festivals in the world. This early edition was just the second major event organized by ID\&T after The Final Exam, a hardcore rave that marked the beginning of the brand's foray into large-scale events. Thunderdome's raw and unfiltered approach to music and culture became a blueprint for hardcore events globally.

Thunderdome quickly grew in popularity throughout the 1990s, establishing itself as the leading force in the hardcore scene. The festival's CD compilations, released in collaboration with Arcade Records and later through ID\&T’s own label, became synonymous with the sound of the genre, helping to spread the music worldwide. By 1995, the Thunderdome brand had sold over 1.5 million compilation albums, making it a cultural phenomenon that reached far beyond the confines of the Netherlands.

The festival's history is marked by several iconic editions and venues, such as the Thunderdome Decade edition in 2002 at the Amsterdam RAI, which celebrated ten years of the brand, and the 2007 edition, held at the Heineken Music Hall, titled Thunderdome XV. Each year brought a unique theme, elaborate stage designs, and legendary DJ performances from artists like Angerfist, DJ Dano, and Buzz Fuzz, who became staples of the Thunderdome experience.

After 20 years of continuous success, the festival held its so-called Final Exam edition in 2012, which was marketed as the last Thunderdome event ever. However, due to overwhelming demand and the enduring legacy of the brand, Thunderdome made a massive comeback in 2017 with its 25th-anniversary edition at the Jaarbeurs in Utrecht. This event was a spectacular return, breaking records with 50,000 attendees and reinforcing its status as the premier hardcore event in the world.

Thunderdome's impact is not limited to its events alone. It has a dedicated fanbase and an entire subculture surrounding it, complete with its own fashion styles, artwork, and a distinctive community spirit. In 2019, a documentary titled Thunderdome Never Dies was released, chronicling the festival’s rich history and cultural significance. The documentary delves into the rise of gabber culture and Thunderdome’s influence on the global hardcore music scene.

Today, Thunderdome continues to push the boundaries of hardcore music, hosting new editions and expanding its presence globally. The festival remains true to its roots, celebrating the hardcore and gabber sound with unmatched energy and a dedication to preserving the history and future of one of dance music’s most intense and beloved genres.
