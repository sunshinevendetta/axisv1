Progressive trance, a subgenre of trance music, emerged in the early 1990s as a response to the more commercial and euphoric styles of trance that were becoming prevalent at the time. Originating primarily in Germany, this genre was heavily influenced by the sounds of house, techno, and ambient music, focusing on creating a more subtle and atmospheric experience compared to the high-energy, anthemic tracks dominating the scene.

The defining characteristics of progressive trance include longer build-ups, gradual layering of sounds, and a focus on creating a journey-like atmosphere in the music. Unlike other trance subgenres, which often rely on dramatic breakdowns and soaring melodies, progressive trance is more about the seamless progression of tracks, often with a darker, deeper, and more hypnotic quality.

Throughout the 1990s, progressive trance coexisted with progressive house, and the two genres were often blended in DJ sets, creating a fluid and evolving musical experience. Labels like Hooj Choons and artists such as Sasha, John Digweed, and BT were instrumental in developing and popularizing progressive trance. Sasha's 1999 track "Xpander" is one of the genre's most iconic tracks, showcasing the deep, layered, and melodic sound that defines progressive trance.

As the genre developed, it maintained a strong underground following, even as other forms of trance and electronic music gained mainstream popularity. The subtlety and depth of progressive trance have allowed it to remain a favorite among fans who appreciate its focus on atmosphere and emotional progression over overt hooks and choruses.

Essential tracks:

- "Xpander" by Sasha: A quintessential progressive trance track that exemplifies the genre's emphasis on deep, evolving soundscapes.
- "Gamemaster" by Lost Tribe: Known for its haunting melodies and gradual build, this track is a classic example of progressive trance.
- "Flaming June" by BT: A track that balances emotional depth with progressive elements, making it a standout in the genre.
