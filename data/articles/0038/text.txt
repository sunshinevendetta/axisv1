**Let’s get into Digifae’s world with NO distractions 🖤✨.**


Welcome to the digital dreamscape of Digifae – a bubblegum bass enigma dripping in neon, heartbreak, and just the right amount of glitchy chaos 💔🌈✨. With an underground following that vibes HARD, they’re bringing a fresh take on hyperpop with lyrics that are both raw and darkly sweet. Get ready to feel like you’re floating in a pixelated love story gone wrong 🕹️.

Essential tracks!:
\- it's love: This track is a cocktail of hurt and allure – like wanting someone who’s clearly bad for you but you can’t look away. Diana Starshine’s voice floats above a laid-back beat, confessing to a toxic connection with “Baby, pour that drink in my cup, tell me that I'm yours, but you just f\*\*\* me up.” It’s giving that hopeless romantic vibe with a bite. Perfect for a night drive when you’re questioning all your choices 👀💔. listen here 🍸😭 [open.spotify.com/track/3H5IPkIEg5JyRZ6h2iA0xr]()

\- 10morekisses: If you’ve ever been stuck on someone who keeps playing with your heart, this song will feel like a gut punch. Recovery Girl and Diana Starshine serve up a bittersweet anthem, with lyrics like “Just ten more kisses 'til I wish I was dead” – so dramatic, yet so relatable. It’s about being trapped in a cycle of love and loss, and that desperation just oozes through every beat. The music itself has a hypnotic beat and a darker, introspective vibe. listen here 💋💔 [open.spotify.com/track/03sGH7v9jUr3kviyqhCVEF]()

\- better: Here, Digifae slows it down a bit with a track that’s all about the struggle to improve for someone you love. Galen Tipton’s soft vocals add a rawness, making the track feel deeply intimate. “I wanna get better for you, better with you,” they sing, over a sweet, almost melancholic beat. This one’s perfect for those days you just wanna sit and vibe with your feelings. It’s got a comforting, nostalgic vibe but still keeps you moving. listen here 💖🖤 [open.spotify.com/track/1pnVD1HyDsDCakwvdZXh5e]()

\- phonecall: Sassy and bold, this track is Digifae calling out someone who just can’t let go. Diana sings with confidence, “Now you still follow me, you want my energy, but I won’t fall down into your hole.” The song has this edgy beat that makes it feel like the ultimate post-breakup anthem. It's one of those "I’m better without you" bops that you’ll wanna blast when you’re ready to let go of someone toxic. listen here 📞🔥 [open.spotify.com/track/58F0w4PwLI5zr6XLuboCF2]()

\- paper knife: This one is a soft, bittersweet track with lyrics like “I’m thinking of you, think I’ll cut you with a paper knife.” It’s about the pain of being with someone who isn’t fully there for you. The sound is bubbly but layered with emotional complexity – you feel the warmth and hurt clashing in every beat. It’s oddly satisfying, like a sad song that feels good to listen to. listen here ✂️💔 [open.spotify.com/track/44sc7mcfQVyCFXPStT1DG7]()

\- lava: This track has a slower, seductive vibe with a warm, liquid feel to it. “Yeah, I’m scared that I might get in too deep” is the lyric that drives the whole mood of the song – it’s all about that fear of getting burned in a relationship that’s all-consuming. \- flowers: Soft and haunting, this track takes you to that post-heartbreak place, where every memory still lingers like a ghost. “I cried a lot and we never even started,” Diana sings, setting the stage for a song that’s as sad as it is beautiful. The tempo is a bit slower, making it perfect for a reflective, late-night listen. It’s all about growing from the pain, like flowers blossoming from heartbreak 🌸💔.
listen here 🌹💀 [open.spotify.com/track/2mCDYaUITZFCGiLvO5y0t8]()
