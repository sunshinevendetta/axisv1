Vaporwave, a subgenre of electronic music, emerged in the early 2010s as a unique blend of nostalgia, critique, and digital art. Born and popularized on the internet, vaporwave is as much a cultural and visual movement as it is a musical genre. It draws heavily from 1980s and 1990s pop culture, incorporating slowed-down samples of smooth jazz, elevator music, R&B, and lounge music. The result is a sound that feels both eerily familiar and strangely dislocated, often creating a dreamy, surreal listening experience.

The genre is characterized by its use of "chopped and screwed" techniques, heavy reverb, and a lo-fi aesthetic that gives the music a retro-futuristic vibe. Vaporwave also frequently critiques consumerism and capitalism, presenting a dystopian yet oddly comforting vision of the past. This is often accompanied by a distinct visual aesthetic that includes retro computer graphics, early internet imagery, glitch art, and neon colors, contributing to its unique cultural identity.

Vaporwave’s origins are deeply tied to the online communities that nurtured it, with platforms like Tumblr, Last.fm, and YouTube playing a crucial role in its spread. The genre's first major release, Floral Shoppe by Macintosh Plus (an alias of Vektroid), is considered a cornerstone of vaporwave, with its iconic track "リサフランク420 / 現代のコンピュー" (often referred to as "Lisa Frank 420 / Modern Computing") becoming emblematic of the genre's sound and aesthetic.

As vaporwave evolved, it began to influence a variety of other artistic and cultural expressions, from visual art to fashion. Despite its niche beginnings, the genre has had a lasting impact, both as a critique of modern consumer culture and as a form of digital nostalgia.

Essential tracks:

- "リサフランク420 / 現代のコンピュー" by Macintosh Plus: A quintessential vaporwave track that epitomizes the genre's use of slowed-down samples and lo-fi aesthetics.
- "Teen Pregnancy" by Blank Banshee: A track from Blank's 0 album, blending vaporwave aesthetics with a futuristic, surreal soundscape that became an anthem for the genre.
- "Ocean Dreams" by Cybereality: A track that embodies the genre's dreamy and surreal atmosphere, offering a perfect representation of vaporwave's nostalgic yet critical essence.
