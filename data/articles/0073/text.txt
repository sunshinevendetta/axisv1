Baile funk, also known as funk carioca, is a genre of music that originated in the favelas of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in the early 1990s. It emerged as a local response to the influence of American hip-hop and electronic music, blending these influences with traditional Brazilian rhythms and cultural elements.

The genre's name, "baile funk," reflects its roots in the "bailes," or parties, that were common in Rio's favelas. Funk carioca is characterized by its energetic beats, catchy hooks, and rhythmic vocal delivery. It often features a mix of rap and singing, with lyrics addressing a range of social issues, from party life to struggles with poverty.

The early sound of baile funk was heavily influenced by Miami bass and Miami bass-influenced hip-hop, with its use of heavy bass lines and rhythmic beats. Pioneering producers such as DJ Marlboro and the group Bonde do Tigrão were instrumental in shaping the genre's distinctive sound, incorporating elements of samba, pagode, and other Brazilian music styles into their tracks.

Throughout the 2000s, baile funk gained wider recognition beyond Brazil, thanks in part to the internet and international collaborations. Artists like Diplo and M.I.A. began to incorporate baile funk elements into their music, helping to introduce the genre to a global audience. The genre also saw the rise of "funk ostentação" in the 2010s, a subgenre characterized by its flashy, extravagant lyrics and production.

Despite its growing popularity, baile funk has faced criticism and censorship, particularly regarding its explicit content and associations with crime. However, it remains a vibrant and influential genre within Brazilian music and continues to evolve with new artists and styles.

- "Baile Funk" by DJ Marlboro: A foundational track that encapsulates the early sound of baile funk, featuring heavy bass and rhythmic beats.
- "Cerol na Mão" by Bonde do Tigrão: A quintessential baile funk track with catchy hooks and energetic production, representing the genre's rise in the early 2000s.
- "Bonde das Maravilhas" by Bonde das Maravilhas: A notable example of funk ostentação, showcasing the genre's evolution with its flashy lyrics and upbeat production.
