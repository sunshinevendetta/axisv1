Phonk is a subgenre of hip-hop that originated in the Southern United States in the early 2010s. It draws heavy influence from 1990s Memphis rap, characterized by its lo-fi production, gritty sound, and use of samples from older rap records, funk, jazz, and soul. The genre often features chopped and screwed vocal samples, eerie melodies, and heavy basslines, creating a dark, atmospheric sound that resonates with a sense of nostalgia for the raw, underground hip-hop of the 1990s.

The roots of phonk can be traced back to the underground hip-hop scene of Memphis, Tennessee, where artists like DJ Screw, Three 6 Mafia, and Tommy Wright III produced music with dark, minimalist beats and lyrics focused on street life, crime, and the occult. Phonk artists in the 2010s began to incorporate these elements into their music, using modern production techniques to create a blend of old-school Southern rap and contemporary sounds.

Phonk's rise in popularity was significantly boosted by the internet, particularly through platforms like SoundCloud and YouTube, where producers and artists could share their work with a global audience. Artists like SpaceGhostPurrp, DJ Smokey, and $uicideboy$ played a key role in popularizing the genre, blending the gritty aesthetic of 90s Memphis rap with elements of trap, lo-fi, and cloud rap.

The genre is often associated with a DIY ethic, with many phonk producers using software like FL Studio to create their tracks, and releasing music independently on digital platforms. Its lo-fi, sample-heavy style and use of analog sounds contrast with the polished production found in mainstream hip-hop, giving phonk a raw, unfiltered edge.

Phonk continues to evolve, with modern artists incorporating more diverse influences and production techniques while maintaining the genre's distinctive dark and atmospheric vibe. Its underground ethos and nostalgic sound have helped it maintain a dedicated fan base, particularly among listeners who appreciate the genre's homage to the early days of Southern hip-hop.

Essential tracks:

- "Mystikal Maze" by DJ Smokey: A track that encapsulates the dark, sample-heavy production and eerie atmosphere typical of phonk music.
- "Pheel tha Phonk" by Soudiere: Known for its lo-fi beats and chopped vocal samples, this track highlights the genre's blend of old-school and modern hip-hop elements.
- "Southside Suicide" by $uicideboy$ & Pouya: A collaboration that combines the gritty, raw energy of phonk with trap influences, showcasing the genre's versatility.
