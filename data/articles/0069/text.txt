Ferry Corsten, born on December 4, 1973, in Rotterdam, Netherlands, is one of the most influential figures in trance music. He began DJing at the age of 15, inspired by Dutch radio DJs like Ferry Maat and Ben Liebrand. Early in his career, Corsten experimented with different aliases, producing hardcore and gabber tracks before transitioning into trance and house music. His first significant breakthrough came in 1996 under the alias Moonman with the track Don’t Be Afraid, which charted in the UK and signaled his arrival on the European dance scene.

In 1999, Corsten released one of his most iconic tracks, Out of the Blue, under his System F alias. This track was a game-changer for trance, achieving international success and landing in the UK Top 20. Around the same time, Corsten also formed Gouryella with fellow Dutch DJ Tiësto, releasing hits like Gouryella and Walhalla, which became anthems in the trance world. He later continued Gouryella as a solo project after Tiësto's departure, releasing Ligaya in 2002.

Corsten's remixing talents became highly sought after, with notable works like William Orbit's Barber’s Adagio for Strings and Silence by Delerium featuring Sarah McLachlan. He also launched Vandit Records and became known for his Trance Nation compilations with Ministry of Sound.

Throughout the 2000s, Corsten produced more trance hits, including collaborations like Exhale with Armin van Buuren and Fire featuring Simon Le Bon of Duran Duran. His 2003 album Right of Way and the follow-up, L.E.F. in 2006, displayed his evolving style, blending electro and house elements with trance.

In 2015, Corsten revived Gouryella with the release of Anahera, which became one of the biggest trance tracks of the year. He continued to push boundaries with the What The F tour, a live performance concept that combined all his major aliases like System F and Gouryella into one immersive show.

Today, Corsten remains one of the most respected names in the electronic music scene, continuously releasing music under his various aliases, performing worldwide, and contributing to the evolution of trance and electronic music as a whole.
