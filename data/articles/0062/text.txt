MUTEK was founded in 2000 by Alain Mongeau in Montreal, Canada, with the goal of creating a platform that showcased cutting-edge electronic music and digital arts. The festival began as a small, innovative event focusing on live performances, experimental electronic sounds, and digital creativity. From the very beginning, MUTEK differentiated itself from other electronic music festivals by emphasizing live sets and collaborative artistic performances rather than DJ sets. The name MUTEK is a combination of "mutation" and "technology," reflecting its mission to evolve and adapt within the ever-changing landscape of digital arts and music.

The first edition of MUTEK in 2000 featured a modest lineup of around 30 artists, including Canadian talents like Tim Hecker and Akufen, who have since gained international recognition. The festival's programming included performances, panels, and discussions that aimed to foster dialogue and exchange between artists, industry professionals, and audiences. MUTEK quickly became a space for experimentation and exploration, encouraging collaborations between musicians and visual artists. This unique approach solidified its position as a leading platform for avant-garde electronic music and digital art in North America.

After several successful editions in Montreal, MUTEK began to expand its reach internationally, starting with Latin America. MUTEK launched its first international edition in Argentina in 2002, followed by Chile in 2003 and Mexico in 2004. These Latin American editions provided new opportunities for local and international artists to connect and collaborate, building a strong community of electronic music and digital art enthusiasts across the region. MUTEK Mexico, in particular, became a pivotal event for electronic music and digital arts in Latin America, celebrating its 20th anniversary in 2024.

The expansion continued with editions in cities like Barcelona in 2009, Tokyo in 2016, and Dubai in 2017, as well as smaller experimental "Micro MUTEK" events in various cities around the world. Each international edition of MUTEK retains the core values and mission of the original Montreal festival but adapts to the unique cultural and artistic contexts of its host city. This global network has helped MUTEK become a significant cultural phenomenon, fostering a sense of unity and shared creativity among artists and audiences worldwide.

Throughout its history, MUTEK has remained committed to its original mission of promoting live performance and innovation in electronic music and digital art. In 2014, MUTEK partnered with Montreal's Elektra festival to create the EM15 event, further blending music with digital arts and expanding its scope. This partnership highlighted MUTEK’s ongoing dedication to exploring new formats and evolving its programming.

MUTEK has also played a crucial role in the careers of many Canadian and international artists. Local Montreal artists like Akufen, Scott Monteith, and Herman Kolgen have used MUTEK as a launchpad to gain wider recognition. The festival’s influence extends beyond North America, providing a platform for artists from around the world to present their work in new and dynamic contexts.

Despite facing challenges such as the economic downturn in the mid-2000s and logistical difficulties with international expansions, MUTEK has managed to sustain its vision and growth. The festival’s resilience and adaptability have allowed it to maintain its relevance and continue to push boundaries in electronic music and digital arts. The organization has embraced new technologies and formats, including virtual editions during the COVID-19 pandemic, ensuring that the spirit of MUTEK remains strong even in difficult times.

MUTEK’s legacy is one of fostering creativity, innovation, and community. Its emphasis on live performance and experimental programming has made it a key player in the evolution of electronic music and digital arts. Today, MUTEK remains one of the most respected and influential festivals of its kind, continuing to inspire and shape the global landscape of electronic music and digital creativity.
