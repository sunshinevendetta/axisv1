Privilege Ibiza, often touted as the world's largest nightclub, has a storied history that reflects the evolution of Ibiza's nightlife. The club’s origins date back to the 1970s when it was known as Club San Rafael. At that time, it was a modest venue featuring a community swimming pool. However, the location’s potential as a nightlife destination was soon recognized, and it began to host parties and events that attracted a growing number of visitors.

In 1983, the club underwent a significant transformation and was rebranded as Ku, named after the restaurant that occupied the site. Ku quickly gained a reputation for its extravagant parties, drawing celebrities and artists from around the world. The club's open-air design, with its massive dance floor and impressive stage, allowed for spectacular performances and shows. This period saw Ku become synonymous with hedonism and excess, epitomizing the wild spirit of Ibiza's nightlife.

Throughout the 1980s, Ku hosted legendary events that left an indelible mark on the island’s party scene. One of the most famous moments in the club’s history was Freddie Mercury's impromptu performance in 1987. He sang "Barcelona" with Montserrat Caballé, marking a historic collaboration that added to the club's mythos.

The transition to Privilege occurred in the early 1990s as the club sought to modernize and expand its appeal. This rebranding marked the beginning of a new era. Privilege retained the grandiosity of Ku but introduced more structured and innovative events. The club’s capacity, capable of holding up to 10,000 people, solidified its status as the world's largest nightclub, a title recognized by the Guinness World Records.

In the 1990s, Privilege embraced the burgeoning electronic music scene. It became a central venue for the island’s dance music revolution, hosting some of the most significant events and residencies. Manumission, one of the most famous and debauched parties in Ibiza’s history, found its home at Privilege. Manumission was known for its outrageous performances, theatrical shows, and a blend of music that kept revelers dancing until dawn. These parties attracted a diverse crowd, from celebrities to regular partygoers, all drawn by the promise of an unforgettable night.

As the 2000s approached, Privilege continued to innovate and adapt. The club hosted an array of events that showcased a wide variety of electronic music genres. This period saw residencies and performances by top-tier DJs and artists such as Tiësto, who held a summer residency that became one of the club’s highlights. These nights drew thousands of fans and contributed to Privilege’s reputation as a must-visit destination for electronic music lovers.

The 2010s brought further evolution, with Privilege expanding its musical offerings and enhancing its production capabilities. The club introduced cutting-edge sound and lighting systems, ensuring that each event provided an immersive and high-energy experience. Collaborations with other prominent nightlife brands and festivals, such as SuperMartXé and Elrow, brought new energy and creativity to the club’s programming.

Privilege’s legacy extends beyond its size and grandiosity. The club has played a crucial role in shaping the global perception of Ibiza as the ultimate party destination. Its influence on the island's tourism and nightlife industries is profound, attracting visitors from around the world who come to experience its legendary nights.

However, the club faced challenges in the late 2010s. In 2018, an inspection revealed illegal structural modifications, leading to partial closures for safety reasons. Privilege officially closed its doors in 2019, and since then, it has been undergoing a significant transformation. In 2022, Grupo Empresas Matutes acquired full ownership of the club and initiated a major renovation project, backed by €8.2 million in government funding. These renovations aim to modernize the club with sustainable technologies, including solar energy systems, and improve its infrastructure.

The reopening of Privilege is anticipated to bring a new era of entertainment to Ibiza, revitalizing the island's nightlife scene. Expected to return by 2025, the club promises to continue its legacy of extravagance and innovation, attracting a global audience of music and nightlife enthusiasts.

Privilege’s journey from a community pool to the world's largest nightclub is a testament to the transformative power of vision and creativity. Its influence on the music and nightlife scenes is profound, having shaped the global perception of Ibiza as the ultimate party destination. Despite its closure, the legacy of Privilege continues to resonate, and its anticipated rebirth holds the promise of new and unforgettable experiences for future generations of partygoers.
