Electroclash is a subgenre of electronic music that blends elements of 1980s electro, new wave, synth-pop, and punk with 1990s techno and house music. It emerged in the late 1990s, primarily in New York City and Europe, and quickly became a significant cultural phenomenon in the early 2000s. Electroclash is known for its retro-futuristic sound, heavy use of synthesizers, drum machines, distorted vocals, and an overall DIY ethos that combines music with fashion and performance art.

The genre is often described as an energetic fusion of underground music and art, characterized by a tongue-in-cheek attitude, provocative lyrics, and minimalist, distorted sounds. Artists like Fischerspooner, Miss Kittin & The Hacker, and Peaches were among the most influential figures in shaping electroclash, bringing its blend of irony and glamour to nightclubs and festivals. DJ Larry Tee, who coined the term "Electroclash," was also instrumental in organizing the first Electroclash Festival in New York in 2001, which further solidified the genre’s prominence.

Electroclash gained a reputation for its crossover appeal, combining dance music with rock influences and performance art. It was not just about music but a complete visual and aesthetic package, embracing neon colors, metallic fabrics, and avant-garde fashion, all of which contributed to its strong cultural impact in the early 2000s.

The genre’s influence began to fade by the mid-2000s, with many elements of electroclash blending into more mainstream pop and electro house music. However, it left a lasting legacy, influencing modern electronic subgenres and artists who continue to draw on its punk-inspired, DIY approach to music and visuals.

Essential tracks:

- "Emerge" by Fischerspooner: An iconic track that epitomizes the electroclash sound with its infectious beats and provocative lyrics.
- "Frank Sinatra" by Miss Kittin & The Hacker: A minimalist, retro-synth track that captures the essence of electroclash's rebellious spirit.
- "Fuck the Pain Away" by Peaches: A bold, raunchy anthem that became one of the most recognizable tracks in the electroclash movement.
