Tijs Michiel Verwest, known as Tiësto, was born on January 17, 1969, in Breda, Netherlands. He began DJing at the age of 14 and built his early reputation by playing at small local clubs, particularly The Spock in Breda. Here, he honed his style, initially mixing new beat and acid house, which laid the foundation for his future trance sound.

In the early 90s, Tiësto released hardcore and gabber tracks under aliases like Da Joker and DJ Limited. His big break came in 1994 when he signed to Basic Beat Recordings. During this period, he teamed up with Arny Bink, and in 1997, they founded Black Hole Recordings, the label that would propel him to global fame. Before his massive success with the Magik series, Tiësto curated the Forbidden Paradise compilations. These early mixes, particularly Forbidden Paradise 3 The Quest for Atlantis 1995 and Forbidden Paradise 6 Valley of Fire 1996, showcased his evolving trance style and helped build his reputation in the European trance community.

The launch of his Magik series in 1997, under Black Hole Recordings, marked a turning point. The series established him as a key figure in the trance scene, and by 1999, Tiësto had partnered with fellow Dutch DJ Ferry Corsten to create Gouryella. Their singles Gouryella and Walhalla became iconic trance tracks, solidifying Tiësto’s status in the electronic music world. Around this time, Tiësto also debuted his In Search of Sunrise series, starting with the first edition in 1999, further establishing his melodic and uplifting trance sound.

In 2000, Tiësto reached new heights with his release of Summerbreeze, a compilation that included his now-legendary remix of Delerium’s Silence featuring Sarah McLachlan. The remix catapulted him into mainstream success, especially in the United States. This momentum carried forward with his debut studio album, In My Memory 2001, which featured some of his most iconic tracks, including Suburban Train, Lethal Industry, and Flight 643. These tracks became staples of his live sets and helped solidify his global appeal.

Tiësto's next major milestone was his second studio album, Just Be 2004, which included hits like Traffic, Love Comes Again, and the title track Just Be. These tracks became anthems in the trance world and showcased Tiësto's ability to craft dancefloor hits. During this period, Tiësto also held residencies at clubs like Amnesia in Ibiza, a key location for his career during the early 2000s.

In 2004, Tiësto became the first DJ to perform at the Olympic Games, playing live at the opening ceremony in Athens. His performance marked a historic moment, both for Tiësto and the broader electronic music scene, as he brought electronic music to a global audience.

In the mid-2000s, Tiësto began exploring new sounds, moving from trance towards progressive house and other genres. His 2007 album Elements of Life marked a shift, reaching number one on the Billboard Top Electronic Albums chart. By this time, Tiësto had become one of the biggest names in electronic music, headlining festivals such as Ultra Music Festival and Tomorrowland.

In 2009, Tiësto sold his stake in Black Hole Recordings and founded Musical Freedom, a label that allowed him to explore more commercial electronic music sounds. His album Kaleidoscope 2009 further pushed him away from his trance roots and towards a more accessible pop-electronic fusion. Collaborations with mainstream artists like Nelly Furtado on Who Wants to Be Alone and the electro-house hit Feel It In My Bones with Tegan and Sara demonstrated his evolving sound.

In recent years, Tiësto has continued to dominate the electronic music scene, with albums like A Town Called Paradise 2014 and The London Sessions 2020. He also won a Grammy in 2015 for Best Remixed Recording with his remix of John Legend's All of Me. Despite moving away from his trance origins, his early works like Forbidden Paradise, Magik, and In Search of Sunrise remain classics that defined an era of trance music and solidified Tiësto’s legacy as one of the greatest electronic music pioneers.

Between 2020 and 2024, Tiësto continued to release chart-topping singles such as The Business 2020, Don't Be Shy 2021 featuring Karol G, and 10 35 2022. These tracks showcased his adaptability, blending electronic music with pop elements, and solidifying his influence across multiple generations of electronic music fans.
