Qlimax, one of the premier hardstyle events in the world, was launched by Q-dance in 2000. Over the years, it has become a cornerstone event for the harder styles of electronic music, featuring some of the most innovative stage designs, anthems, and audiovisual storytelling experiences. Initially, Qlimax was not exclusively focused on hardstyle. Early lineups included artists from hard trance and hard house genres, such as Armin van Buuren and Ferry Corsten, but the festival gradually shifted its focus to hardstyle and hardcore music by 2003.

Held annually at the GelreDome in Arnhem, Netherlands, Qlimax has developed a reputation for its theatrical themes, intricate stage designs, and unforgettable visual effects. Each edition revolves around a central theme, with top-tier artists creating exclusive anthems that capture the essence of that year's storyline. DJs like Headhunterz, Technoboy, and The Prophet have been regular fixtures at Qlimax, with Technoboy holding the record for the most performances.

Qlimax’s history has also included several international editions, with events in Belgium in 2006 and 2007. The brand has experimented with multiple editions per year, but since 2004, it has focused on a single, highly-anticipated annual edition. Despite interruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, Qlimax adapted by hosting virtual events, showcasing its dedication to the community.

In 2024, Q-dance announced that Qlimax would be hosting its final edition titled "The Final Prophecy," marking the end of an era for the festival. This decision comes as part of a broader shift within Q-dance’s event strategy, following the cancellation of other major events like Qapital and X-Qlusive.
