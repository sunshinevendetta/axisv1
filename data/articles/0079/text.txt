Reggaeton, a genre that has become a global musical phenomenon, originated in the late 1980s and early 1990s, with its roots deeply embedded in Latin America, particularly in Panama and Puerto Rico. The genre is a fusion of various musical styles, including Jamaican dancehall, reggae en español, hip-hop, and Latin American rhythms.

The genre's early development is credited to the influence of Jamaican immigrants in Panama, who brought reggae music with them. This influence led to the creation of reggae en español, a precursor to reggaeton. However, it was in Puerto Rico where reggaeton truly took shape as a distinct genre. In the early 1990s, underground music scenes in Puerto Rico began experimenting with these sounds, blending them with hip-hop beats and Spanish-language lyrics. This new sound was initially called "underground" or "perreo," and was distributed through mixtapes and live performances in clubs and carports.

By the mid-1990s, reggaeton had evolved into a more defined genre, characterized by its signature dembow rhythm—a beat derived from Jamaican dancehall, particularly the track "Dem Bow" by Shabba Ranks. Producers like DJ Playero and DJ Nelson played crucial roles in shaping the early sound of reggaeton, which was raw and often focused on themes of urban life, including poverty, violence, and social struggles.

Reggaeton began to gain mainstream recognition in the early 2000s, thanks in part to artists like Daddy Yankee, Don Omar, and Tego Calderón. Daddy Yankee's 2004 hit "Gasolina" became a global sensation, helping to propel reggaeton onto the international stage. The genre's catchy beats and infectious hooks made it a staple in clubs around the world.

The rise of the internet and social media further fueled reggaeton's popularity, allowing artists to reach wider audiences and collaborate with international stars. Reggaeton's influence continued to grow, blending with other genres like hip-hop, R&B, and electronic music, and evolving into subgenres like Latin trap.

Reggaeton remains one of the most influential and popular genres in Latin music today, with its sound continuing to evolve and its artists dominating global music charts.

Essential tracks:

- "Gasolina" by Daddy Yankee: A defining track that brought reggaeton to a global audience, known for its catchy dembow rhythm and energetic delivery.
- "Dile" by Don Omar: A classic example of early 2000s reggaeton, blending hip-hop influences with the genre's distinctive beat.
- "Tu Pun Pun" by El General: One of the early tracks that helped lay the foundation for reggaeton, combining reggae en español with a dancehall vibe.
