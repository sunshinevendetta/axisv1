Chill-out music, often simply referred to as "chillout," is a subgenre of electronic music that emerged in the early 1990s, primarily as a counterpoint to the fast-paced, high-energy sounds of rave and house music. It is characterized by its slow tempos, relaxed beats, and ambient soundscapes, making it perfect for unwinding and relaxation. The genre blends elements of ambient, downtempo, lounge, and jazz, often incorporating soft melodies, smooth basslines, and soothing rhythms.

The origins of chill-out can be traced back to the "chillout rooms" of nightclubs in the UK, where ravers would retreat to relax and recover from the intensity of the main dance floors. These spaces played ambient and downtempo music, offering a calming atmosphere that contrasted with the high-energy beats of the main room. The genre soon gained popularity outside of these settings, finding its way into cafes, lounges, and personal listening experiences.

Chill-out music often draws on a wide range of influences, including world music, classical, and soft rock. Artists like Moby, Air, and Thievery Corporation helped popularize the genre in the 1990s and 2000s, blending electronic production with organic instruments and creating music that was both soothing and sophisticated.

Chill-out compilations like the "Café del Mar" series became iconic in the genre, offering listeners carefully curated selections of tracks that captured the essence of the chill-out vibe. These compilations were often associated with the Balearic Islands, particularly Ibiza, where the sunset sessions at Café del Mar became legendary for their relaxed, blissful atmosphere.

Essential tracks:

- "Porcelain" by Moby: A quintessential chill-out track with its dreamy melodies and smooth production, embodying the serene essence of the genre.
- "La Femme d'Argent" by Air: A smooth, flowing track that blends electronic and organic elements, creating a relaxed and immersive listening experience.
- "Lebanese Blonde" by Thievery Corporation: Known for its fusion of downtempo beats and Middle Eastern influences, this track is a staple in the chill-out genre.
