🔥 OSNO1: The Glitchcore Phantom That Became a Hyperpop Queen 🔥

Alright, let's dive into the sonic chaos that is OSNO1, aka Laura Les, aka one-half of the legendary 100 gecs! This is where it all started before she became the nightcore vocal deity we know today. OSNO1 was a whole vibe—raw, experimental, and soaked in early hyperpop energy. This is the underground blueprint, the DNA of everything that came after. 🌀💿

🌟 Tags That Define the Energy:

Glitchcore? Check. Digicore? Yup. Electronic weirdness? Absolutely. OSNO1 was the moment before hyperpop exploded—screeching synths, distorted vocals, and lyrics that feel like an internet fever dream. Imagine if a Tumblr-era emo kid got thrown into a blender with a Vocaloid and a broken Game Boy. That’s the OSNO1 sound. 🎮⚡

🎧 Essential Tracks!:

\- A Conversation I Overheard: This one’s a full-on mind trip. The lyrics paint a surreal dinner table drama, laced with paranoia and obsession, all wrapped in a twisted electronic lullaby. It’s both eerie and hypnotic, pulling you into a glitchy dreamscape. listen here 🎵➡️ <https://open.spotify.com/track/7jUa6Loc6TGsmXqxpawkvK>

\- I'm Sorry: Pure emotional chaos. Layers of autotuned vocals, distorted beats, and unexpected sample flips create a rollercoaster of sound. This track feels like a hacked video game soundtrack with all the glitches turned up to 100. listen here 🎵➡️ <https://open.spotify.com/track/2WTf7B0kaG5F1recPgBfvX>

👥 Who’s In Her Orbit?

Before she was crafting chaotic bangers with Dylan Brady as 100 gecs, Laura Les was building her own universe of experimental electronic madness. OSNO1’s sound laid the foundation for what hyperpop would become. If you’re into artists like Fraxiom, Jane Remover, or Gupi, this is your sacred text. 🛸🔮

💎 Dive Into Her Universe:

The OSNO1 era was all about DIY digital punk energy. You can hear the early gecs DNA in every track—pitched-up vocals that sound like they’re screaming through a dial-up connection, beats that hit like an internet meltdown, and lyrics that make you feel like you’ve been sucked into a late-night forum post from 2013. This is the sound of hyperpop before it had a name. 🎶💥

🌐 Related Stars Shining Bright:

\- Dorian Electra: Gender-fluid hyperpop chaos wrapped in Baroque synths and medieval aesthetics. If OSNO1 made you feel something, Dorian will take you to another dimension. ⚔️

\- Food House: Fraxiom and Gupi’s unhinged hyperpop lovechild. Think broken synths, high-speed beats, and a refusal to take anything seriously. 🍔

\- Machine Girl: The noisier, more aggressive sibling of hyperpop. If OSNO1’s glitchcore vibes hit, Machine Girl’s breakcore assault is the next step. 💀

Ready to experience OSNO1 and witness the origins of a hyperpop legend? This is where it all began. Plug in, glitch out, and let the digital distortion take over. 🚀💿
